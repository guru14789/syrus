About Our Project

SYRUS CHATBOT represents a cutting-edge solution that integrates natural language processing (NLP) and computer vision to create an advanced image recognition chatbot. Our goal is to provide users with a chatbot that can analyze and interpret images, generating accurate and contextually relevant responses to user queries about the image.

Introduction
With the rise of artificial intelligence (AI) and computer vision, creating conversational models that combine both NLP and image recognition remains a challenge. SYRUS CHATBOT aims to bridge this gap by offering a chatbot that not only processes natural language but also understands and analyzes images. By leveraging technologies like YOLOv3 for object detection and Mask R-CNN for instance segmentation, our chatbot can accurately recognize objects within images and generate responses based on the detected objects.

Related Work
Traditional chatbots often rely on hard-coded templates or deep learning models to generate responses. While deep learning models can generate grammatically correct responses, they often lack memory of previous interactions. SYRUS CHATBOT utilizes a Late Fusion Encoder model, combining image features with historical dialog data to provide more accurate and contextually relevant responses. We also incorporate state-of-the-art object detection methods, such as YOLO and Mask R-CNN, to enhance our chatbot's ability to interpret and respond to visual data.

Approach
The SYRUS CHATBOT employs an encoder-decoder framework. The encoder processes both image and text inputs, while the decoder generates responses based on the encoded information. The Late Fusion Encoder combines features from the image and text inputs into a joint representation. Our approach also includes a Self-Attention module to ensure that the chatbot effectively utilizes all available information for generating responses.

Experiments
Our experimental setup involves training the chatbot using the COCO dataset, which provides annotated images for object detection and segmentation. The training process includes data preprocessing, hyperparameter tuning, and evaluation using metrics such as Mean Reciprocal Rank (MRR) and Recall@k. Our results demonstrate the effectiveness of combining image and text features for improved chatbot performance.

Conclusion
SYRUS CHATBOT showcases the potential of integrating NLP and computer vision technologies to create a powerful image recognition chatbot. By leveraging advanced models and datasets, we aim to provide users with a tool that can effectively interpret and respond to queries about images, making it a valuable asset in various domains such as organizations, educational institutions, and more.
